Firewall Overview

Firewall Basics

A firewall is a system designed to prevent unauthorized access to a private network by filtering incoming information from the internet. It acts as a barrier, blocking unwanted traffic and permitting only desired traffic.

Purpose of a Firewall

  • Creates a safety barrier between a private and the public internet to prevent unauthorized access.
  • Allows active monitoring and management of network access.
  • Protects against spyware, hackers, and other cyber threats.
  • Preserves the privacy of devices or networks.
  • Enables the establishment and implementation of security policies and rules.

Types of Firewalls

Firewalls can have different structures, functionality, and traffic-filtering methods. Here are some common types:

  • Packet Filtering Firewalls: Operate at junction points like routers and switches, checking data packets against established criteria.
  • Proxy-Level or Application-Level Firewalls: Operate at the application level, using pre-configured policies to manage messages.
  • Stateful Inspection Firewalls: Evaluate data packets based on a database of previous successful connections, offering greater security but potentially impacting network performance.
  • Next-Generation Firewalls (NGFWs): Combine various methods, monitoring packets at an application level and conducting quick network assessments to detect suspicious or invasive activity.
